What is Robinhood?

The company that changed how America invests

🏹

The Robin Hood of Finance

Robinhood Markets, Inc. is an American financial services company headquartered in Menlo Park, California. Incorporated in 2013 and launched to the public in 2015, it operates a commission-free investing platform that democratized access to stock markets for the everyday American.

The company's name is inspired by the legendary outlaw Robin Hood, who stole from the rich to give to the poor — symbolizing their mission to take sophisticated financial tools that were once only available to the wealthy and institutional investors, and put them in the hands of ordinary people.

Robinhood pioneered zero-commission trading, which eventually forced all major US brokerages — including Charles Schwab, E*Trade, and TD Ameritrade — to eliminate their own fees by 2019, fundamentally reshaping the entire brokerage industry.

Founder Story

The Two Immigrants Who Took on Wall Street

From Bulgarian and Indian roots to a $32 billion empire

VT

Vlad Tenev

Co-Founder & CEO
🇧🇬 Born in Bulgaria, raised in Virginia, USA

Vladislav Tenev was born in Varna, Bulgaria, and moved to the United States as a child when his parents — both economists — relocated to work for the World Bank in Washington D.C. Growing up in suburban Virginia, he excelled academically before heading to Stanford.

At Stanford, he studied mathematics and physics, where he met his future co-founder. After graduation, he pursued a mathematics PhD at UCLA before dropping out to co-found his first startup with Baiju Bhatt.

Prior to Robinhood, Vlad and Baiju co-founded Celeris and Chronos Research — companies that sold high-frequency trading software to hedge funds and Wall Street institutions. This gave them deep insight into the massive gap between institutional trading costs (near zero) and retail investor costs ($5–$10 per trade).

"We built trading systems for hedge funds and saw firsthand that the financial industry was designed for the wealthy. We wanted to change that."
— Vlad Tenev
BB

Baiju Bhatt

Co-Founder & Former Co-CEO
🇮🇳 Indian origin, raised in Virginia, USA

Baiju Bhatt was born in the United States to Indian immigrant parents who came to America to pursue PhDs. His parents settled in Virginia, giving him a remarkably similar background to his future co-founder — both were only children of immigrant scientists who grew up in the same state.

Baiju studied physics and mathematics at Stanford University, where he and Vlad became roommates, best friends, and late-night problem-set partners. Their bond was cemented over chess games, gym sessions, and a shared obsession with building things that matter.

After leaving Robinhood's day-to-day operations, Bhatt focused on other creative endeavors — reflecting his multidisciplinary curiosity that also shaped Robinhood's design-forward culture.

"At Stanford, you're surrounded by people building things that change the world. We wanted to democratize access to the markets — make investing as simple as sending a text."
— Baiju Bhatt

🎓 The Origin Story: From Stanford to Wall Street to Silicon Valley

01

Stanford Meeting (2005)

Vlad and Baiju meet during summer 2005 as undergraduates at Stanford University. Both children of immigrants, both studying mathematics and physics — they immediately bond over their shared worldview and curiosity.

02

Wall Street Experience (2010–2012)

After graduating, they move to New York City and launch two fintech companies selling ultra-fast trading algorithms to hedge funds. They witness firsthand that Wall Street firms pay pennies per trade while retail investors pay $10+.

03

The Occupy Wall Street Moment (2011)

The Occupy Wall Street movement inspires them. The 2008 financial crisis had exposed the wealth gap. They ask: "Why can't ordinary people access the same markets and tools as billionaires?" The seed of Robinhood is planted.

04

The Name "Robinhood" (2013)

They name the company after the legendary outlaw who redistributed wealth. Their tagline: "Democratize finance for all." They incorporate Robinhood Markets, Inc. on April 18, 2013 in Menlo Park, California.

05

Waitlist Launch (December 2013)

They launch a simple landing page with a waitlist. Within a day, over 10,000 people sign up. By the time they launch the app, nearly 1 million people are on the waiting list — validation that the world was ready.

Launches cash management. Series D and E funding reaches $1.3B total raised, valuing the company at $7.6 billion. In October, Charles Schwab, TD Ameritrade, and E*Trade all drop trading commissions to zero — directly because of Robinhood's pressure.

COVID-19 pandemic triggers a massive wave of retail investing. Robinhood adds 3 million new accounts in Q1 2020 alone. Total users surpass 13 million. A critical outage during market volatility triggers regulatory scrutiny.

Robinhood goes public on NASDAQ under the ticker "HOOD" on July 29, 2021, at $38/share, valuing the company at approximately $32 billion. 25% of IPO shares were reserved for retail investors on the Robinhood platform itself.

Business Model

How Robinhood Makes Money

The "free" platform that generates billions through clever financial engineering

Robinhood's genius lies in its counterintuitive proposition: charge users nothing, yet generate billions in revenue. The platform offers zero-commission trading, but has built sophisticated revenue streams that most users never see. This model forced the entire US brokerage industry to restructure.

01
🔄

Payment for Order Flow (PFOF)

Primary Revenue Stream

When a Robinhood user places a trade, that order is routed to a market maker (like Citadel Securities or Virtu Financial) rather than directly to a stock exchange. The market maker executes the trade and pays Robinhood a small rebate — typically fractions of a cent per share.

While each rebate is tiny, millions of daily trades add up to hundreds of millions of dollars annually.

User places order → Robinhood routes to market maker → Market maker pays rebate → User gets trade executed
⚠️ Controversial: Critics argue PFOF creates a conflict of interest. The EU has banned PFOF, affecting Robinhood's international model.

🏦

Net Interest Income

Robinhood earns interest in multiple ways:

  • Margin Lending: Charges interest when users borrow to trade on margin
  • Cash Sweeps: Sweeps uninvested cash to partner banks and earns the spread
  • Securities Lending: Lends customer shares to short sellers
Largest segment in 2024–25

Crypto Transaction Revenue

Robinhood earns a spread on cryptocurrency trades — the difference between buy and sell prices. This spread-based model generates significant revenue as crypto volumes grow, especially during bull markets.

Challenges & Controversies

The Dark Side of Disruption

No revolutionary company comes without controversy

🚨 Major

The GameStop (GME) Crisis — January 2021

In late January 2021, retail investors on Reddit's r/WallStreetBets coordinated to buy GameStop (GME) shares to squeeze short-sellers. The stock skyrocketed from ~$20 to nearly $500.

Robinhood abruptly restricted buying of GME and other "meme stocks" — while still allowing selling. The stated reason was to meet NSCC clearinghouse deposit requirements (which ballooned to $3 billion overnight).

The backlash was enormous: users accused Robinhood of siding with hedge funds, class-action lawsuits were filed, and CEO Vlad Tenev was hauled before Congress to testify.

Outcome: FINRA fined Robinhood $57M in 2021, the largest FINRA fine at the time. Multiple lawsuits settled. Prompted SEC investigation into PFOF practices.
⚠️ Significant

Payment for Order Flow (PFOF) Ethics

PFOF — Robinhood's primary revenue mechanism — has been criticized as a conflict of interest. Critics argue that routing orders to the highest-paying market maker, rather than the one offering the best price, may result in worse execution prices for retail users.

Outcome: SEC settled a $65M PFOF-related enforcement in 2020. Ongoing regulatory debate continues globally.
⚠️ Significant

March 2020 Trading Outages

During the historic COVID-19 market crash in March 2020, Robinhood's platform experienced multiple prolonged outages at precisely the moment users needed access most. Thousands of traders lost money.

Outcome: Class-action lawsuits. FINRA fined Robinhood $70M for outages, misleading customers, and approving risky options trades for inexperienced traders.
⚠️ Significant

"Gamification" of Investing Criticism

Robinhood's confetti animations (removed in 2021), push notifications celebrating trades, and game-like UI were criticized for encouraging impulsive, risky trading — especially among young, inexperienced investors.

Outcome: Robinhood removed gamification features in 2021 and improved options disclosures after regulatory pressure.
🔴 Regulatory

Crypto & Securities Regulatory Issues

Robinhood Crypto was charged by the SEC in 2024 for operating as an unregistered securities exchange. Additionally, some cryptocurrencies offered on the platform were alleged to be unregistered securities.

Outcome: Charges partially dropped after the SEC's stance on crypto softened under the new administration in 2025.
📉 Corporate

2022 Layoffs & Stock Crash

After the 2021 IPO euphoria, Robinhood's stock crashed from $85 to under $7 by mid-2022 as trading volumes dropped in the bear market. The company laid off ~23% (780 employees) in April 2022 and another 9% (150 employees) in August 2022.

Outcome: Cost-cutting and diversification helped Robinhood return to profitability by 2023–2024.

Future Outlook

The Road Ahead

Robinhood's vision for the next decade of finance

🌍

Global Expansion

Robinhood is aggressively expanding internationally through its Bitstamp acquisition and organic growth. Target markets include UK, Singapore, Australia, and eventually emerging markets across Asia and Latin America. The goal: become the world's largest retail investment platform.

🤖

AI-Powered Finance

Robinhood is integrating AI across its platform — from AI-powered trade recommendations to "agentic" trading bots that can execute strategies automatically. The Legend platform serves as the testing ground for these professional-grade AI tools.

💰

The Great Wealth Transfer

An estimated $84 trillion in wealth will pass from Baby Boomers to Millennials and Gen-Z over the next 20 years. Robinhood is strategically positioning itself to capture this historic transfer — with younger generations already deeply embedded in its ecosystem.

🏦

Full Banking Platform

Robinhood is moving toward becoming a full-service bank — with checking, savings, credit cards, mortgages, and wealth management all under one roof. The TradePMR acquisition is a key step in capturing high-net-worth advisory relationships.

📈

Futures & Derivatives Growth

Expanding futures trading (commodities, indices, FX, crypto) with varying leverage levels, particularly in EU markets. This positions Robinhood to compete with CME Group and traditional futures brokers.

🔐

Regulatory Navigation

As crypto regulations mature under the new US framework and international rules evolve, Robinhood is investing heavily in compliance infrastructure. A clearer regulatory environment could unlock significant new products and markets.

Fintech Impact

How Robinhood Changed Finance

The lasting legacy of America's most disruptive brokerage

$0

Eliminated Commissions Industry-Wide

By 2019, Robinhood's pressure forced every major US brokerage to drop trading fees to zero — saving American retail investors an estimated $1 billion+ per year in commissions.

50M+

New Investors Entered the Market

Studies show that tens of millions of first-time investors opened their first brokerage account on Robinhood — dramatically expanding stock market participation among underrepresented demographics.

$1

Fractional Share Revolution

By enabling fractional share investing from as little as $1, Robinhood made it possible for low-income investors to own shares of Amazon, Google, and Tesla.

📱

Mobile-First Investing Norm

Robinhood proved that investing could be as simple as checking social media. Its mobile-first approach forced the entire industry to redesign their apps for younger, smartphone-native investors.

₿

Mainstreamed Crypto for Retail

Robinhood was among the first major regulated platforms to offer free cryptocurrency trading, helping to mainstream Bitcoin and Ethereum adoption among retail investors.

🏛️

Sparked Regulatory Reform

The GameStop saga triggered Congressional hearings on PFOF, market structure, and the power of retail investors — conversations that continue to shape US financial regulation today.
